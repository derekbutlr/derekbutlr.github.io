### How would you describe stage and commit to your non-tech-savvy friend? Say you are writing your speech in class, and you've got some parts of it you are unsure of. You take these parts up to the teacher for review (staging branch) to see if they fit with the rest of your speech. # Branch, Pull, Merge This is a great tool to allow working with others on a project pretty seamlessly.
### What is Master? The branch that is at the top of the tree. Or the top of the river- it is the most upstream so to speak. It's the best version of your project that you can create experimental branches off of without directly affecting it unless you want it to.

### Why create a Branch? It's a great way to try out new ideas without messing with your already-working stuff that may wreak havoc if you accidentally delete something, so you can have peace of mind. It's a great way to collaborate with others too, with each of you working on different branches of the same master file that you can merge together when you are ready to- or nitpick beforehand if need be.

### Do the concepts introduced feel intuitive or difficult to understand? It is a little bit tricky to wrap my head aroumd, but with the real-world analogies and good examples it's something I think I will get the hang of with further use.

# Thinking like a programmer
### Describe the process for solving problems - Start your timer. - Approach the question and see if you really understand it. - Make a plan for how you will tackle it. - Subjugate your plan into doable chunks. - Begin tackling! Write the steps you take. - Get unstuck (coming back to the problem after some coffee can never hurt) - Build on what you've accomplished & improve it. - Go over what you learned (Reflect)

### Will you follow that process? How? I've been keeping a google doc separate to reflections that I've been putting up images where I get stuck, and trying to write how I fixed it afterward. It gives me a little bit more motivation to keep trying new solutions whenever I look at it!


### What did you learn about flipped classroom? It can be a little bit daunting when you are learning as you encounter problems, but figuring out how to solve those problems and applying it will give you skills that'll be useful -so you'll retain those skills- rather than stuffing a bunch of information that you don't need at the current time for anything in particular into your brain. It also means that everyone can learn at their own pace, while still getting the help that they need if they can't figure it out on their own.
