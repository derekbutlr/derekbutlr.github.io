Identity, Values, and Strengths

I was born in Auckland. As the youngest child of 5 growing up with just me and my mother, times were pretty tough sometimes. I got a laptop for my birthday when I was very young, and immediately started playing multiplayer games on it (runescape, wow, all the life-stealing ones!) and I spent the majority of my time on it. I really wanted to make my own runescape back then!

Growing up we moved a lot. And I mean a lot. I went to two different primary schools, intermediates, and high schools. This shaped who I am drastically, for better or for worse. I met a lot of new people & gained a lot of life-long relationships, and it gave me more perspective about how important these relationships actually are.

An experience that I would describe as a milestone of my life would be when I had the decision of moving with my mother or staying at my current location during the years of my 2nd high school. I chose the latter. I had had enough of shifting all around the place and wanted to focus on the relationships that I had- and myself. It meant that I was a lot more distanced with my family, but I retained and strengthened the bonds that I had with my pals.

It’s not something that I regret to this day as I have managed to maintain these relationships well despite living far away from everyone at this point!

Another experience would be when I tried out an automotive course.

I like cars & pretty much anything with a motor a LOT , so I thought it could be a good career! I quickly came to learn that once you spend all day working on other peoples’ vehicles & not your own, you don’t really want to go home and spend all night wrenching. I actually learned a lot in this course despite not wanting to continue in that line of work, and now I’ve got a bit stronger of an intuition for fixing things; having had a couple of project cars and bikes since then. Its more of a passion-hobby for me rather than something I want to spend the rest of my life doing- so I ended up dropping out after a few months. This gave me a bit of a push to start exploring career options and really think about what I would like to spend the rest of my life doing- it took me a while, trying out different jobs here and there- but now I’ve landed at Dev Academy. And I’m having a fantastic time learning.

For an example of working with others I’d use when my pal and I had a shared project bike. I wanted it to have a black frame, he wanted it to have a blue frame. We compromised by flipping a coin! It was a little more fair than either of us choosing, and we were both happy with the outcome once it was painted.

One of my strengths but I’d say once of my biggest weaknesses is my preference to work alone. I haven’t really participated in many group projects thus far- although I’d love to! Since I struggle with communicating sometimes it can be a little difficult to get my ideas across, so I tend to just apply them myself. I’d love to work on improving in this aspect and I know it only comes with getting outside of my comfort zone.
