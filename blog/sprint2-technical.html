What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Margin is how much space is around an object.
Padding is how much space is within an object sides and it’s contents.
Border is between the margin and padding of an object. Only counted when given an actual width otherwise, it’s zero.

What matters is defining width. If you have a house (x) metres long – (y) metres wide – a garden and fence surrounding it- and you are looking at it from a birds’ eye view. You can imagine inside the house as the content, the garden is the padding, and the fence is the border. A margin is the space outside of something, if you didn’t want the footpath outside your garden to be within an inch of your fence, you would give it a margin of 1 inch.

What is a box model in CSS?

The box model defines CSS dimension properties of an HTML element.

The box model is made up of four primary styling hooks: margin, border, padding, and width/height.
Think of it like a series of boxes (block-level elements) which contain, and can contain others which all have these properties.
When nested, the children elements such as div, span, h1, and so on—take on and are modified by the margin, border, padding, and h/w of their ancestors, and such is their placement.
A parent can define a style which a child can either inherit, or define itself.

A little more clarification!

Margin: It lies outside the box and is basically used to separate itself from other markup elements.
Border: It is the border of our box, that also lies outside but before margin.
Padding: Lies inside of the box, creates inner space in the box when used. It lies after the border.
Height/Width: The dimensions of the actual box content and the amount of space that they take.
